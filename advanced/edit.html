Points, lines and angles are the basics of geometry which collectively define the shapes of an object. An example of a combination of points, lines and angles is a rectangle which has four vertices defined by a point, four sides shown by lines and four angles equal to 90 degrees. Similarly, we can define other shapes such as rhombus, parallelogram, square, kite, cube, cuboid, etc using these three primary figures.
